# From Mathematics to Code - My Journey

I spent years in math olympiads before I touched my first line of production code. People often ask if that background helped or hindered my transition to software engineering.

The honest answer? Both.

## What Math Gave Me

**Pattern recognition.** Every algorithm is just a structured way to spot patterns and act on them. Years of finding elegant solutions to geometry problems made me hungry for clean abstractions in code.

**Patience with complexity.** Spending three hours on a single proof teaches you to sit with problems. Bugs that would frustrate others feel like puzzles to me - just another locked door.

**Rigorous thinking.** Math taught me to question assumptions. "Why does this work?" matters as much as "Does this work?"

## What I Had to Unlearn

**Perfection is the enemy.** In math, there's one right answer. In software, "good enough, shipped today" beats "perfect, shipped never."

**Solo work isn't enough.** Math competitions are individual. Engineering is a team sport. I had to learn to ask for help, give feedback, and write code that others could read.

**Tools matter.** I spent too long hand-rolling things I should have used libraries for. Sometimes the elegant solution is `npm install`.

## The Bridge

What surprised me most: the same instincts that helped me with math help me with code. Decomposition, looking for invariants, finding the smallest case that breaks.

The biggest difference is the feedback loop. In math, you might work for weeks before knowing if you're right. In code, you can write a test in minutes and *know*.

That fast feedback is addictive. It's why I haven't looked back.
